Top 5 Must-See Alpine Villages

The Alps are dotted with countless charming villages, but some are true standouts. Here are our top five picks for the most picturesque, must-see alpine villages in the DACH region.

1. Hallstatt, Austria

Nestled between a serene lake and towering mountains, Hallstatt is the definition of a fairytale village. Its 16th-century alpine houses and charming market square make it one of the most photographed spots in Austria. Take the funicular up to the Skywalk for breathtaking panoramic views.

2. Wengen, Switzerland

A car-free village perched on a sunny terrace above the Lauterbrunnen valley, Wengen offers classic Swiss charm and spectacular views of the Jungfrau massif. It's a paradise for hikers in the summer and skiers in the winter, accessible only by a scenic cogwheel train.

3. Garmisch-Partenkirchen, Germany

Located at the foot of Germany's highest peak, the Zugspitze, this Bavarian town is a hub for outdoor adventures. Stroll through its historic streets adorned with 'Lüftlmalerei' (fresco paintings) and enjoy the lively atmosphere of a classic German mountain resort.

4. Zermatt, Switzerland

Famous for its iconic view of the Matterhorn, Zermatt is another car-free village that combines rustic charm with world-class amenities. It's a mecca for mountaineers, skiers, and anyone who appreciates dramatic alpine scenery.

5. Alpbach, Austria

Voted "Austria's Most Beautiful Village," Alpbach is renowned for its uniform wooden architectural style and stunning floral displays in summer. It's a quieter, more traditional alternative to some of the busier resorts, offering an authentic Tyrolean experience.
